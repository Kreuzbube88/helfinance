# Getting Started

## Welcome to HELFINANCE! 👋

HELFINANCE helps you keep track of your finances — no cloud, no subscription, no data sharing. Everything stays on your own server.

In the next few minutes you'll set up your first income sources and expenses. After that, the dashboard will immediately show you where you stand financially.

---

## Onboarding: Guided Setup

On your first login, HELFINANCE starts a short onboarding process. It guides you step by step through the basic setup:

1. **Add your first income** — e.g. your salary
2. **Add your first expense** — e.g. rent or electricity
3. **View the dashboard** — your first financial overview

> ✅ You can skip the onboarding at any time and enter data manually later.

---

## Dashboard Overview

After onboarding, you'll see the dashboard with four widgets:

### 🩺 Health Score

A value from 0–100 that summarizes your financial health.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 90–100 | Excellent — you save more than you spend |
| 70–89 | Good — solid finances with room to improve |
| 50–69 | Fair — watch your expenses |
| Below 50 | Warning — expenses exceed income |

### 🚦 Traffic Light

Shows at a glance whether your budget is in the green zone:

- 🟢 **Green** — income significantly higher than expenses
- 🟡 **Yellow** — tight budget, expenses close to income level
- 🔴 **Red** — expenses exceed income

### 💰 Free Money

What you have left over this month:

```
Free Money = Income − Expenses − Loan Payments
```

Example: Salary $3,500 − Rent $1,200 − Utilities $150 − Car loan $350 = **$1,800 free**

### 📅 Upcoming Bookings

The next upcoming transactions, sorted by date. So you can see at a glance what's due in the coming days.

## Recommended Setup Order

For the best results, we recommend this order:

- [ ] **1. Income** — add all income sources (salary, freelance, child benefits…)
- [ ] **2. Fixed expenses** — rent, electricity, internet, insurance…
- [ ] **3. Variable expenses** — groceries, leisure, clothing…
- [ ] **4. Loans** — car loan, personal loan…
- [ ] **5. Savings** (optional) — enter your current savings account balance

## Tips for Beginners

### Understanding the Booking Day

The booking day is the date when money moves — so the day of the debit or salary payment.

✅ Salary arrives on the 15th → Booking day: `15`
✅ Rent is debited on the 1st → Booking day: `1`
❌ Not: using mid-month as the booking day for everything

### Interval Types

| Interval | When to use | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Monthly | Fixed monthly amounts | Rent $1,200 |
| Quarterly | Every 3 months | Insurance $180/quarter |
| Semi-annual | Every 6 months | License fee split |
| Yearly | Once a year | Car registration $240/year |

> ⚠️ **Important:** Annual expenses like car registration should be entered as **yearly** (not monthly with 1/12 of the amount). HELFINANCE calculates the monthly portion automatically.

### Categories

Categories help you analyze expenses in reports. Examples:

- Housing (rent, utilities, internet)
- Transportation (car, gas, public transit)
- Groceries
- Leisure & hobbies
- Insurance
- Health

> ✅ Tip: Don't create too many categories. 5–8 categories are enough for a good overview.

### Valid From / Until

Every booking can have a **validity period**:

- **Valid from**: From when does this booking apply? (Default: today)
- **Valid until**: Until when? Leave blank = indefinitely

Example: Phone contract expires December 2025 → `Valid until: 12/31/2025`

---

## Avoid Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong approach | ✅ Correct |
|------------------|-----------|
| Car registration $240 as **monthly** $240 | Car registration $240 as **yearly** |
| Entering salary on the 1st when it arrives on the 15th | Set booking day to the actual arrival date |
| A new category for every single expense | Max. 8 categories, group similar ones |
| Groceries without a category | Always choose a category (for reports) |
